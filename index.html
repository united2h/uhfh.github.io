United Harmonies for Humanity

United Harmonies for Humanity (Uhfh / @united2h) is an Iowa non-profit 501(c)(3) organization built on two core pillars: Humanity and Music. It connects community care, music education, and purposeful performance through one shared mission.

The organization serves as a bridge between humanitarian service and musical growth, creating meaningful programs for public, private, and care-centered settings.

Who We Are

A non-profit organization where humanity and music work together

United Harmonies for Humanity uses music as a path for compassion, education, connection, and service. Its two core units work together continuously: the Humanity Unit serves community needs, and the Music Unit develops learning, mentoring, and performance.

Our Purpose

“Harmonizing people through music for humanitarian causes, we organize events to raise awareness, funds, and goods for charity, acting as a bridge to connect music with various non-profit organizations.”

Care-Centered Outreach

Special emphasis is placed on hospice patients, elder centers, transitional care communities, special kids, and new mothers. Music is offered as comfort, reassurance, calm, joy, and human connection where it is needed most.

The Humanity Unit also strengthens collaboration with partner organizations to support inclusive and dignified service.

Music Unit

Teaching, mentoring, and performance aligned with humanitarian purpose

The Music Unit builds musical skill, discipline, and service-minded performance. It includes the School of Music and the performance-focused United Harmonies Band (UHB).

Prominent Music Unit Program

United Harmonies Band (UHB)

UHB is the organization’s lead student ensemble, made up of middle school and high school students trained in their instruments and guided by professional mentorship.

The group performs uplifting multicultural selections, light Indian-inspired melodies, and popular western tunes at public and private events, with music always connected to compassion and humanitarian service.

UHB performs at 10+ public events in a year and is available for indoor, outdoor, public, private, scheduled, and on-demand programs. Students also coach other performers through continual internal mentoring.

School of Music

The School of Music offers early and developing instruction in vocal music, violin, piano, keyboard, percussion, and performance skills.

Training supports confidence, discipline, mentoring, and service-minded musicianship.
